The first request was denied by our re solution of September 14, 1946, The remaining four will presently be. taken up.

Of course, there is no dispute that the commitment order was appealable. (Section 10, Rule 64}.  It appears, however, that Agustin Dayoan1s notice of appeal was late, for, as pointed out by the trial judge, it shoulcl have been submitted fifteen clays after advice of the commitment order which was clone on. June 8, 1946. (Sec. 10, Hule 64In connection with Sec. 6, Rule 116)

As to the petitioner's request for fixing his bail, it is clear that the Fiscal’s action, squarely met it. Anyway, his right to appeal having fizzled out, there is no occasion for any appeal bond. Heedless to add, his other requests have no factual nor legal basis.

He contends, however, that he rosy not properly be kept indefinitely in prison for failure to deliver unto Segundo Dayoan thirteen (13) uyones of palay, for the reason that, he having disposed of the cereal, his situation does not fall within the scope of section 7 of Rule 64 providing that "when the contempt consists in the omission to do an act which is yet in the power of the accused to perform, he may be imprisoned x x x until he performs it."

On this point our opinion is that his ability or in ability to perform was undoubtedly estimated by the commit ting judicial officer; and petitioner having neglected to take the indispensable steps to bring up his case for re view, this Court is not in a position to revise the question of fact involved, and must yield to the presumption of validity of the order. And although it must be conceded, as he argues, that he may be discharged from custody "when it appears that public interest will not suffer thereby" (Section 9, Rule 64) still, such deliverance does not rest in the discretion of this Supreme Bench but in the power of the lower court, that undoubtedly could now validly exercise its discretion in his favor considering; first, the time he has served, second, the rule was technical that deprived him of a review, and third, the circumstance that substantial compliance with the directive he has disobeyed may perhaps be obtained by a writ of execution.

The petitions will be denied. No costs.

Dissenting Opinion

Perfecto, J.:

In Civil Case No. 2817 of the Court of First Instance of Ilocos Sur, Judge Simeon Ramos issued on October 25, 1944, an order the dispositive part of which is as follows:

 

POR TODO LO EXPUESTO, el Juzgado ordena que Agustin Dayoan oese en su cargo de deposi tario y q.ue presente la cuenta final de su car go, cuenta que debera ser clara y detallada, con los comprobantes necesarios.  

 

SE ORDENA ADEMAS que el demandante puede ya tomar parte en la administraeion de los terrenos y percibir los frutos de Ios mismos en la poroidn indioada en la decision, previnien dose a los demandados no impidan al deraandante a tomar parte en la administracion y percibir frutos que le correspondan.  

On January 15, 1946, a complaint was filed against petitioner for contempt in alleged violation of the above order.

On May 18, 1946, respondent Judge Manuel Blanco rendered a judgment the pertinent parts of which we quote:

 

De las pruebas practicadas el Juzgado en cuentra establecido q.ue los recurridos ilgustin Dayoan y Marianito Ipelari, en sus respectivas calidades de depositario el primero y administrador especial el segundo, administraban 42 de los terrenos objeto de este asunto, los cuales produjeron el año 1945 un canon equivalente a ochenta uyones de palay para el dueño de los terrenos, que en la proporcion de 1/6 parte en algunos de ellos y 5/12 avas partes en otros que ostenta el dernafidante segun la decision final y firme en este asunto, le corresponderian trece (13) uyones de palay, los cuales los recurridos Dayoan e Ipalari retienen y no han entregado al demandante, en contravencion de la orden de este Juzgado de 25 de octubre de 1944 y de los requerimientos que por escrito les ha hecho aquel."  

 

.    .    .    .    .    .    . 

 

Los hechos arriba expuestos son, en sentir del que provee, constitutivos de tin desacato a este Juzgado, previsto en el inciso (b) del Articulo 3 y penado en el Articulo 7, ambos de la Regla Judicial No. 64.  

 

POR TANTO, se ordena el arresto y reclusion de Agustin Dayoan y Marianito Ipalari hasta que entreguen al demandante Segundo Dayoan los trece (13) uyones de palay arriba mencionadas.  

There is nothing to show, upon the facts delcared proved in the judgment, that petitioner had violated the order of October 25, 1944, wherein any order to the effect that Agustin Dayoan should, jointly with Marianito Ipalari, deliver 13 uyones of palay to Segundo Dayoan does not appear.

On June 12, 1946, petitioner, who by virtue of the judgment was confined in the provincial Jail of Ilocos Sur, filed a petition to be bailed in order that he may secure the services of an attorney. On June 16, 1946, sustaining the opposition of the adverse party, Judge Blanco denied the petition.

On June 3, 1945, petitioner filed a pleading appealing against the judgment.

Petitioner complains that he has been under arrest since June 5, 1946, but on said date he could not get any information as to the contents of the judgment, not withstanding his insistent desire to read it, the record cleric alleging that the record was in the possession of the judge.  It was only on June 8, 1946, that he was notified of the judgment.

Petitioner alleges that he has consumed all the small amount of palay (part only of the 13 uyones in question) he is ordered in the judgment to deliver and, therefore, he is not in a position to perform the delivery.

The petitioner appears to be one of those unfortunate victims of self-delusion who, because of a smattering or superficial knowledge of law, believe themselves fully competent to handle their own case without the assistance of a lawyer, with the result that they bungle miserably their own case.  To this must be added the handicap that petitioner was held in prison even before he filed the petition and did not enjoy the freedom to handle his own case the best way he can or to appear before us and submit all the papers needed in support of his petition.

The situation imposes upon our own sense of justice the exertion of an especial endeavor to understand and to do justice.

From the pleadings of both petitioner and respondents and the annexes submitted by the latter, we can glean that petitioner appears to have been adjudged guilty of contempt for alleged disobedience in the performance of a duty not specifically enjoined upon him; that he appealed and his right of appeal was denied; that he prayed to be bailed in order to look for an attorney to handle his case, and his prayer was denied; that he is being kept in jail until he delivers part of the 13 uyones of palay which had been in his possession but which he can not deliver anymore because he had consumed them all. There are various evidence or, at least, strong indications that petitioner is a victim of a miscarriage of justice; and. there is nothing to justify us in closing our eyes and giving deaf ears to the plaints coming from the provincial jail of llocos Sur.

We are of opinion, and so vote, that petitioner must immediately be released in order to enable him to properly handle his case or, at least, that he be granted provisional release on bail in the amount of P100.00, and that the record of Civil Case No. 2817, of the Court of First Instance of llocos Sur, be ordered forwarded to this Court so we may do what justice demands.
