A.M. No. 14-10-322-RTC, December 05, 2017

RE: HABITUAL ABSENTEEISM OF RABINDRANATH A. TUZON, OFFICER-IN-CHARGE (OIC)/COURT LEGAL RESEARCHER II, BRANCH 91, REGIONAL TRIAL COURT, BALER, AURORA

RESOLUTION


PER CURIAM:

<div align="justify">A Report<sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[1]</sup> submitted by the Leave Division, Office of the Court Administrator (<em>OCA</em>) dated 16 October 2014, shows that respondent Rabindranath A. Tuzon (<em>Tuzon</em>), OIC/Legal Researcher II, Branch 91, Regional Trial Court (<em>RTC</em>), Baler, Aurora, had incurred unauthorized absences for the months of June to November 2013, as follows: <blockquote> <table width="66%" cellpadding="0" border="0"> <tbody><tr valign="top"> <td width="45%">June 3-4, 13-14 <br> August 5-8, 15, 28<br> September 2-5, 9-13, 26 <br> October 7-9, 14, 16-18, 21 <br> November 4-6, 14 </td> <td width="55%">4.0 days <br> 6.0 days <br> 10.0 days<br> 8.0 days <br> 4.0 days </td> </tr> </tbody></table> </blockquote> <p><strong>On 13 November 2014, the OCA issued a 1st Indorsement</strong><sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[2]</sup> <strong>directing Tuzon to comment on the aforesaid report. However, he failed to comply with the said directive, thus, on 10 August 2015, the OCA issued a Tracer<sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[3]</sup> reiterating its earlier directive for him to file a comment. No comment has been filed to this date. </strong> </p> <p> On 27 June 2016, the Court, in A.M. No. 16-04-88-RTC,<sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[4]</sup> issued a Resolution dropping Tuzon from the rolls effective 1 March 2014, for having been on absence without official leave (<em>AWOL</em>). The resolution held that respondent Tuzon "<em>is still qualified to receive any benefit that he may be entitled to under existing laws and be re-employed in the government, without prejudice to the outcome</em>" of the present case, i.e., A.M. No. 14-10- 322-RTC. </p><p> In an agenda report dated 10 April 2017, the OCA recommended that: </p><table width="100%" cellpadding="0" border="0"> <tbody><tr valign="top"> <td width="6%"><div align="center">(1) </div></td> <td width="94%"><div align="justify">the Report dated 16 October 2014 of Mr. Ryan U. Lopez, Officer-In-Charge, Employees' Leave Division, Office of Administrative Services, Office of the Court Administrator, be <strong>RE-DOCKETED</strong> as a regular administrative matter against respondent Rabindranath A. Tuzon, OIC/Court Legal Researcher II, Branch 91, Regional Trial Court, Baler, Aurora, for <em>Habitual Absenteeism</em>; and </div></td> </tr> <tr valign="top"> <td><br></td> <td><br></td> </tr> <tr valign="top"> <td><div align="center">(2)</div></td> <td> <div align="justify">respondent Tuzon be found <strong>GUILTY </strong>of Habitual Absenteeism; and </div></td> </tr> <tr valign="top"> <td><br></td> <td><br></td> </tr> <tr valign="top"> <td><div align="center">(3) </div></td> <td><div align="justify">accordingly, since respondent Tuzon has been dropped from the rolls, the following<strong> ACCESSORY PENALTIES</strong> may be imposed on him: <strong>CANCELLATION OF ELIGIBILITY, FORFEITURE OF RETIREMENT BENEFITS, PERPETUAL DISQUALIFICATION OF HOLDING PUBLIC OFFICE AND BAR FROM TAKING CIVIL SERVICE EXAMINATIONS. </strong></div></td> </tr> </tbody></table> <p>We adopt the findings of the OCA. </p><p> Administrative Circular No. 14-2002<sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[5]</sup> it is provides that: </p><blockquote> <p> An officer or employee in the civil service shall be considered habitually absent if he incurs unauthorized absences exceeding the allowable 2.5 days monthly leave credits under the law for at least three (3) months in a semester or at least three (3) consecutive months during the year.<sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[6]</sup> </p> </blockquote> <p> We have often held that, by reason of the nature and functions of their office, officials and employees of the Judiciary must be role models in the faithful observance of the constitutional canon that public office is a public trust. Inherent in this mandate is the observance of prescribed office hours and the efficient use of every moment thereof for public service, if only to recompense the Government, and ultimately, the people who shoulder the cost of maintaining the Judiciary. Thus, to inspire public respect for the justice system, court officials and employees are at all times behooved to strictly observe official time. As punctuality is a virtue, absenteeism and tardiness are impermissible.<sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[7]</sup> </p><p> Since Tuzon was been absent for 4 days in June, 6 days in August, 10 days in September, 8 days in October, and 4 days in November 2013, there is no dispute that he had been habitually absent. </p><p> Administrative Circular No. 14-2002 and <em>The Uniform Rules on Administrative Cases in the Civil Service</em> impose the penalty of suspension of 6 months and 1 day to 1 year, for the first offense, and dismissal, for the second offense, in case of frequent unauthorized absences. However, in the determination of the penalty to be imposed, attendant circumstances, such as physical fitness, habituality, and length of service in the government, may be considered.<sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[8]</sup> </p><p> <strong>Here, it is noteworthy to stress that the OCA report shows that the Court, in prior resolutions, had penalized Tuzon with a reprimand for his habitual tardiness,</strong><sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[9]</sup> <strong>and with a six-month suspension for grave misconduct.</strong><sup style="color: rgb(255, 0, 0);">[10]</sup> <strong>Hence, we cannot find any circumstance which can mitigate the imposable penalty. </strong> </p><p> <strong>WHEREFORE</strong>, the Court finds respondent Rabindranath A. Tuzon, OIC/Legal Researcher II, Branch 91, Regional Trial Court, Baler, Aurora, <strong>GUILTY</strong> of habitual absenteeism. He is hereby ordered <strong>DISMISSED </strong>from the service, with forfeiture of all retirement benefits, except for any accrued leave credits; cancellation of eligibility, bar from taking civil service examinations, and with prejudice to re-employment in any government branch or instrumentality, including government-owned or-controlled corporations.</p><p><strong>SO ORDERED.</strong> </p><p> <em>Sereno, C.J., Carpio, Velasco, Jr., Leonardo-De Castro, Peralta, Del Castillo, Perlas-Bernabe, Leonen, Caguioa, Martires, Tijam</em>, and <em>Reyes, Jr., JJ</em>., concur. <br> <em>Bersamin, J</em>., on official leave. <br> <em>Jardeleza</em> and <em>Gesmundo, JJ.,</em> on leave. <br><br></p><p> </p><p> </p><hr width="60%" size="1" noshade="noshade" align="center"> <p align="center"><strong><br> NOTICE OF JUDGMENT</strong></p> <p>Sirs/Mesdames:</p> <p>Please take notice that on <strong><u>December 5, 2017</u></strong> a Decision/<u>Resolution</u>, copy attached herewith, was rendered by the Supreme Court in the above-entitled administrative matter, the original of which was received by this Office on January 24, 2018, at 10:02 a.m.</p> <table width="100%" cellpadding="0" border="0"> <tbody><tr> <td width="69%">&nbsp;</td> <td width="31%"><p align="center">Very truly yours,</p> <p align="center"><strong>(SGD.)
