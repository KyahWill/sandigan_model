159-A Phil. 523

SECOND DIVISION

[ Adm. Matter No. 810-CJ, May 30, 1975

JOSE KUAN SING, PETITIONER, VS. JUDGE ROSENDO BALTAZAR, RESPONDENT.

R E S O L U T I O N


BARREDO, J.:

After a review of the record, the Court believes that at the most, respondent may have committed an error of judgment.  There is no clear showing of bad faith on his part.

Complainant was the offended party in a case of serious physical injuries thru reckless imprudence, Criminal Case No. 47207; entitled People of the Philippines vs. Edgar Jarantilla.  The prosecution tried to prove that complainant was sideswiped by the car of accused Jarantilla.  On the other hand, the defense presented evidence tending to show that it was not the vehicle of said accused that hit him.  Evaluating the evidence, respondent judge found for the accused.  We have read the decision, and We cannot see any indication that the same is the product of a prejudiced mind.

The circumstances pointed to by complainant regarding the past relationship of respondent with Mayor Rodolfo Ganzon of Iloilo City and his direct or indirect intervention in the case do not to Our mind sufficiently establish that respondent has acted without regard to the high standards required of him as a judge.  Charges against judges for rendering malicious decisions must be proven by strong and convincing evidence.  Otherwise, judges would be at the mercy of disgruntled litigants against whom adverse actions might have to be taken.

WHEREFORE, the complaint is dismissed.

Fernando, Chairman, Antonio, Aquino and Concepcion Jr., JJ., concur.
